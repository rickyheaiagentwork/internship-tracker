## Overview: Bio + AI is Exploding

This sector is growing fastest in AI — AI-discovered drugs are entering clinical trials, precision medicine is becoming standard of care, and biotech valuations have doubled since 2023. The talent gap means strong opportunities for students who can bridge CS/ML with biology.

### What You'd Actually Do

#### Drug Discovery (#28, 31, 32)
- **Build generative models for molecular design** — create novel molecules with desired properties
- **Train GNNs on protein structures** — predict how proteins fold and interact
- **Accelerate target identification** — use ML to find which genes/drugs to study next
- Insilico has already brought an AI-designed drug into Phase I clinical trials

#### Precision Medicine (#27, 30)
- **Analyze genomic data + clinical records** — match treatments to individual patients
- **Process pathology images with CNNs/ViTs** — detect cancer patterns invisible to human eyes
- **Build predictive models for patient outcomes** — help clinicians make better decisions
- Tempus has one of the world's largest clinical datasets (millions of patient profiles)

#### Healthcare AI (#29, 38)
- **Verily**: Health monitoring wearables, drug development platforms, population health AI
- **NVIDIA Clara**: GPU-accelerated medical imaging, genomic analysis pipelines, radiology AI

## Why Bio + AI is Worth It

1. **Massive impact** — AI-discovered drugs could save lives and reduce R&D costs by 60-80%
2. **Less competition than pure SWE** — requires domain knowledge that's harder to learn on your own
3. **Job security** — healthcare/longevity will only grow; recession-resistant industry
4. **Unique edge** — being one of the few who can speak both CS and biology is extremely valuable
5. **Strong funding** — biotech AI attracted $8B+ in VC funding in 2024 alone

## Application Tips for Bio-AI Roles

- **Portfolio projects**: Fine-tune a model on biological data (UniProt, PDB, ClinVar, TCGA)
- **Publications help enormously**: Even poster sessions at CBMS, RECOMB, or ISMB
- **Talk to biologists**: Understand the wet-lab process you're trying to automate/optimize
- **Know the datasets**: TCGA (cancer genomics), PDB (protein structures), MIMIC-III (clinical notes)
- **Consider grad school later**: Many bio-AI researchers have PhDs, but top interns with strong portfolios get hired
